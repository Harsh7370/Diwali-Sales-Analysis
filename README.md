# Diwali Sales Analysis — Exploratory Data Analysis

## Project Overview
This project performs an end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Diwali sales dataset
containing 11,250+ records. The goal is to analyze customer purchasing behavior across demographics,
geography, and product categories to derive actionable business insights.

---

## Tech Stack
- **Language:** Python
- **Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn

---

## Dataset Features
| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| User_ID | Unique customer identifier |
| Gender | Customer gender |
| Age Group | Age bracket of customer |
| State | Customer's state |
| Occupation | Customer's profession |
| Product_Category | Category of purchased product |
| Orders | Number of orders placed |
| Amount | Total purchase amount |

---

## Analysis Performed
- **Gender Analysis** — Purchase count and spending comparison between male and female customers
- **Age Group Analysis** — Identifying the most active and highest-spending age segments
- **State-wise Analysis** — Top states by total orders and revenue
- **Occupation Analysis** — Spending patterns across different professions
- **Product Category Analysis** — Best-selling categories by orders and revenue

---

## Key Insights
- Female customers outnumbered male buyers and had significantly higher purchasing power
- The **26--35 age group** were the most active and highest-spending segment
- **Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka** generated the highest orders and revenue
- Customers in **IT, Healthcare, and Aviation** sectors spent the most
- **Food, Clothing, and Electronics** were the top-selling product categories

---

## Conclusion
> Married women aged 26--35 from Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka, working in IT,
> Healthcare, and Aviation, are the most valuable customer segment during Diwali. Businesses
> should prioritize targeted marketing campaigns toward this demographic, focusing on Food,
> Clothing, and Electronics categories to maximize sales revenue.
